About Reef Architect

Reef Architect is an independent educational and planning platform designed to help those in the reefing community of all experience levels build, visualize, and refine their aquarium systems with clarity and confidence. It combines real-world equipment data, livestock compatibility logic, and visual feedback tools into a single step-by-step builder experience.

The concept for Reef Architect was inspired by the kind of powerful configurators found in the personal-computer community—platforms that let builders plan custom PC setups and check part compatibility before buying. Drawing from that same spirit of accessibility and precision, Reef Architect adapts those user-experience principles for the aquarium hobby. It is an original work built specifically for aquatics, not affiliated with, endorsed by, or derived from any existing PC-building service or brand. All product names, logos, and trademarks referenced on this site remain the property of their respective owners.

The goal is to bring that same do-it-yourself empowerment to reefkeeping and offering a structured, transparent way to plan systems that match real biological and mechanical needs. Every element here, from tank volumes and pump capacities to coral lighting ranges, was created to educate hobbyists on how their choices affect stability and long-term success.

Reef Architect was developed as a labor of passion for the reefing community that I am apart of. The project’s purpose is educational only: to promote safe, sustainable reefkeeping practices and to make technical planning approachable for everyone.

Reef Glossary

Coral Families

  • Soft Corals — Leathers, Zoas, Mushrooms.
  • LPS — Large-Polyp Stony (Hammers, Duncans).
  • SPS — Small-Polyp Stony (Acropora, Montipora).

Core Terms

  • PAR — Light at coral height; target depends on corals.
  • Turnover — Return pump flow ÷ tank gallons (~3–5×/hr).
  • Total Flow — (Return + Powerheads) ÷ gallons.
  • ATO — Auto Top-Off controls salinity.
